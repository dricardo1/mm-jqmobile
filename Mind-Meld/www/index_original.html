HOW TO PLAY

Ask a simple question. Listen. Repeat.

DISCLAIMER

PLAY AT YOUR OWN RISK.

Persons with known psychological or interpersonal relational difficulties should exit the app now. Developers are not resonsible for any lingering psychological effects of gameplay on participants.


RULES

There's 3 sets of 12 cards, totaling 36 questions or tasks. One of you reads the first card aloud. Then, one after the other, both do what it asks, starting with the person who read the card aloud. When you are both done, go to the next card, alternating the first person to read the card. Repeat until blown away.

It's ok if you don't finish all the cards within an hour or so. Take plenty of time with each card, doing what it asks thoroughly and thoughtfully...and now, you may begin!

Question 36


Part I

Share a personal problem and ask your partner's advice on how he or she might handle it. Also, ask your partner to reflect back to you how you seem to be feeling about the problem you have chosen.


Part II

Lastly, take four minutes and without speaking, each of you look into your partner's eyes. Then, you can reflect on how much closer to each other you may now feel and how much you've learned about each other in so short a time. Now, please go share this app with others!

Credits and Links


Formulated by social psychology researcher Dr. Arthur Aron of the Interpersonal Relationships Lab at Stony Brook University in New York, these 36 questions were usually found to make two people feel better about each other and want to see each other again. Dr. Aron published his results in the abstract:
The Experimental Generation of Interpersonal Closeness" in Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin (1997).

This app is an homage to and digital implementation of his team's research, in order that many more may be exposed to and benefit from their work. We hope you've enjoyed and benefited from this app.
